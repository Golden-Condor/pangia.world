Why this belongs here

It adds context, not just opinion.

The Insights section is where Pangia organizes local environmental context into something homeowners can actually use. This report fits that purpose because it captures how people in Western North Carolina talk about water quality, trust public systems, and describe safety concerns in their own terms.

How to use it

Read it as a context layer.

This is not a replacement for utility data, lab testing, or a site-specific Diagnostic. It is a supporting resource. Use it to understand what people are noticing, where trust breaks down, and how community concerns may differ from the language used in formal reports.

Best next step

Pair it with a Pangia brief or a water-specific page.

The most useful way to read this survey is alongside the Asheville tap water article, the water-quality reference page, or a local issue brief. That keeps the survey grounded in a broader evidence and interpretation framework.
